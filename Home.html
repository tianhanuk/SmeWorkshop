SME Policy Workshop

Defeating Uncertainties in the SME Sector through Policy Engagements

  22 - 24 March, 2022

The University of Nottingham joins forces with Bank of England (BoE) and Confederation of British Industry (CBI) to organise an online Small Medium Enterprises (SME) Policy Workshop on 22 -24 March 2022. The focus will be on the challenges and opportunities in the SME sector across Europe, Asia and the globe. The event is supported by Research England’s QR-PSF Funding.

During 3 half-day sessions, we promise to showcase research, hold panel discussions, and deliver training for early career researchers (ECRs) and PhD students on research-for-policy.

The first day of the event will feature the launch of INFINITY – an Inclusive Financial Technology (INFINITY) Innovation Hub based at the University of Nottingham focusing on inclusive finance and fintech research. This will follow a panel and research presentations where we will share our UKRI-funded SME Pandemic Risk research findings with wider audience and discuss global SME challenges with national and international policymakers. The second day features two keynotes sessions, a panel, and showcases of research conducted via industry-university partnerships. The final day is devoted to the training of ECRs and PhD students and features a keynote and two presentations from policy makers.

The workshop has already attracted prominent speakers from LSE, University of Exeter, University of Nottingham, Asian Development Bank Institute, Bank of England, Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE), Bpifrance, British Business Bank, CBI, European Central Bank, Experian, Innovate Finance, Liberis, OECD, UK Finance and the United Nations Industrial Development Organisation (UNIDO).

This event will be vital for entrepreneurs, business analysts, policy makers, researchers, students and the inquisitive public in general to learn more about recent developments on financial inclusion, Fintech, risk assessment for SMEs, empowering SMEs through policies, industrial collaborations, and capacity building for ECRs and PhD students.
